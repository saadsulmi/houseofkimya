Tailoring Essentials

Measurements We Take

01
Bust / Chest
Fullest part of the chest, measured over the bust line.
02
Waist
Natural waist — the narrowest part of your torso.
03
Hip
Fullest part of the hips, about 8–9 inches below the waist.
04
Kurti Length
From shoulder top to desired hem length.
05
Salwar Length
From waist to ankle or preferred length.
06
Sleeve Length
From shoulder point to wrist, with arm slightly bent.
07
Shoulder Width
Across the back from shoulder seam to shoulder seam.
08
Neck
Style preference: round, V-neck, collar, or boat neck.

How It Works

Our Stitching Process

❧
1
Consultation & Style Selection

Bring your inspiration images or describe your dream outfit. We discuss style, occasion, fabric, and embellishment preferences together.

2
Fabric Sourcing & Measurement

Choose from our curated fabric collection or bring your own. We take all 8 key measurements for a perfectly tailored fit.

3
Cutting & Pattern Making

Our tailors draft a custom pattern based on your measurements, then carefully cut the fabric for minimal waste and best drape.

4
Stitching & Detailing

Expert stitching with attention to seam finishes, embroidery placement, pleat formation, and lining as required.

5
Trial Fitting & Alterations

Try on your outfit — we make any adjustments needed to ensure the perfect fit before final finishing.

6
Final Delivery

Your outfit is pressed, packaged, and ready to wear. Delivery time is typically 7–14 days depending on complexity.

The Women Behind the Studio

Meet Our Founders

Two passionate women from the heart of South Kerala, weaving their dreams into every stitch.

❧
JS
Jasna Sidhiq
Co-Founder & Head of Design

Born and raised in Kollam, Kerala, Jasna grew up surrounded by the vivid colours of traditional South Indian textiles — the deep reds of Kasavu borders, the shimmer of Kanchipuram silk, and the breezy softness of Kerala cotton. Fashion was never just clothing to her; it was a language she was born knowing.

"Every woman deserves a garment that feels like it was made just for her — because with us, it truly is."

After years of honing her craft through formal training and countless hours at the sewing machine, Jasna channelled her passion into co-founding House of Kimya. She leads the design vision of the studio — translating each client's personality into silhouettes, necklines, and embellishments that feel both timeless and deeply personal. Her signature touch: garments that honour tradition while feeling entirely contemporary.

SF
Suman Farsna
Co-Founder & Master Tailor

Also from the lush landscapes of South Kerala, Suman Farsna discovered her calling in the precise, meditative art of tailoring. Where others saw fabric, she saw potential — the curve of a sleeve, the fall of a pleat, the way a well-placed dart can transform an ordinary piece into something extraordinary.

"Fashion is not about following trends — it is about crafting something that makes a woman walk taller and feel truly herself."

Suman is the technical backbone of House of Kimya — the one who ensures that Jasna's creative visions are realised with impeccable precision. Her expertise spans pattern-making, draping, and finish-work, from intricate Patiala pleating to the finest bridal embellishments. Together, the two women bring a rare combination of artistry and mastery to every garment they create.

Born in Kerala. Built on Passion.
✦ ✦ ✦

Jasna and Suman are more than business partners — they are two women who dared to follow their love for fashion in a world that often asks women to choose practicality over passion. House of Kimya is their answer: a studio rooted in the rich textile traditions of South Kerala, run entirely by women who understand what it means to want clothing that truly fits — not just the body, but the soul.
